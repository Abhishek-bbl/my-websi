🌄 The Ride Begins

It all started with a 48 km ride to Almatti... I was in my favorite shirt — sleeves folded, untucked — the usual charm I save just for her.

And there she stood, waiting even before I reached — her face gently veiled by the brown kurta’s dupatta, eyes shining, clutching her little backpack like it held all her secrets.

The moment our eyes met... the world slowed down. Everything else blurred into the background.

She climbed onto the bike, without a word — just a quiet smile. Her arms didn’t wrap around me completely... instead, her hand softly rested on my shoulder.

And as the road beneath us broke into humps and turns, her grip tightened — each little squeeze saying things we never dared to speak. In those moments, it wasn’t just her hand… it was her trust, her presence, her love — holding on to me.

🍽️ Breakfast & Butterflies

We reached Bagalkot and stopped at Pakwan — the same old hotel, but this time... it felt different.

“These water bottles are so weird,” she whispered, looking at the label. I just smiled — her voice made everything else feel so quiet.

While she spoke, I secretly texted my friend to bring a surprise — two roses, a KitKat, and a gift she’d never expect.

But she caught me. Snatched my phone, laughed, and called my aunt randomly. That laugh? It echoed in my heart.

She slowly pushed her plate toward me and said, “Cut this vada into 8 pieces for me.” That simple ask... meant everything.

Her bag’s zip got stuck. We both tried, both failed — and ended up giggling like kids. It wasn’t about the zip. It was about us.

🎁 The Gift Surprise & Stroll

Just outside the hotel, my friends walked in — carrying a college bag. Inside it? The surprise I had secretly planned: 2 roses, a KitKat, and a gift I’d kept safe for a year.

I introduced Sneha to them with the biggest smile. She stood there — calm, soft, and sweet — and my heart? Proud. That moment felt like a flex I’d waited years for.

We didn’t open the bag just yet. Instead, we walked together to a nearby gift center — like a couple lost in their own tiny world.

She scanned shelves of pen stands and incense burners. Nothing felt right... until the next shop across the road. There, she finally chose a pen stand and a backflow incense burner. I paid. She smiled. And in that moment, I melted.

We kept the items in my bag and continued — window shopping a bit more before heading to a cosmetic store. She picked conditioner and moisturizer while I watched her glow under soft lights ✨

We sat for a while, again trying to fix her stuck zip. Still no luck. But between that laugh, her soft “ugh,” and our fingers brushing — something clicked better than that zip ever would.

👗 The Dress Hunt at Trends

We reached the Trends showroom, and I walked ahead — opened the glass door like a gentleman. She walked in first, and I followed her, watching the sunlight play with her dupatta.

We began scanning racks, floor by floor. She looked at a few dresses, but nothing seemed to excite her.

I spotted tiny kids’ slippers and joked, “These might just fit you.” She burst out laughing and gently hit my shoulder.

On the first floor, she finally liked one dress — but of course, it wasn’t in her size. She sighed and said, “I already have something like this at home anyway.”

While walking down the stairs, I gently reached over and removed her mask. She looked up with those soft, surprised eyes — and in that second, it was just us.

No selfies. No crowd. No noise. Just a silent heartbeat kind of moment that still echoes in my head.

🍗 Lunch at Amogham

We walked into Amogham and found a cozy table, just the two of us. It was quiet, peaceful — like the world paused so we could breathe in each other.

We ordered chicken biryani and a chicken starter — but the food took forever. So I just sat there, looking at her. She caught me a few times... but didn’t mind.

Her eyes kept going to the surprise bag. “What’s in it?” she asked again and again. But I just smiled. Not yet, I thought. Not yet.

When the food finally arrived, we ate with our hands — no spoons, no formality. Just comfort. Just real.

I fed her a bite of biryani. She looked at me and fed me back. I swear... that was it. That was the moment I fell all over again.

After we finished, I went to wash hands. She dried hers… on my shirt. My favorite shirt. And somehow, it felt more like love than anything.

She sat beside me while we waited for the bill. I shifted my chair back so she could lean. She tried to peek into the surprise bag again — I held it tighter, smiling.

🛣️ Ride to Muddebihal & The Surprise Gifts

It was time to drop her back to Muddebihal. My heart was heavy... the kind of heavy that only comes when a beautiful day is ending.

I placed the surprise bag in front of my bike, right on the petrol tank. And we started riding. Wind on our faces... memories behind us.

For the first 10-15 km, I was holding the rose — fingers tight, heart tighter. I wanted the moment to be perfect.

Finally, I turned slightly, handed her the roses on the moving bike. She gasped, smiled, held them like they were the most precious thing she’d ever received.

Then came the KitKat — simple, sweet, just like her smile when I passed it over my shoulder.

But the final gift… it wasn’t just a surprise. It was a memory, a promise I’d kept for a whole year.

A small bottle of nail polish — the exact shade she had once said she loved. A year ago. And I had saved it... just for this moment.

She went silent. And I could feel her emotions melt into the wind. We didn’t speak — but everything was said.

Then, as the sun slowly dipped and the wind danced around us… I gently reached for her hand.

I held her left hand with my left — riding the bike with just one hand, our fingers locked, hearts aligned.

We stayed like that for the rest of the journey. Her hand in mine, warmth in our silence. That moment... was pure magic.

📸 The Unsaid Goodbye

I dropped her at Muddebihal, and we both sat quietly for a second — not wanting to leave, not ready to let go.

We didn’t hug. We didn’t even take a selfie. It wasn’t because we didn’t want to — we just… didn’t know how to say goodbye.

I kept waiting for her to ask. She kept waiting for me.

Our hearts were heavy, our eyes said everything. And in that silence — we made a quiet promise to meet again.

As I rode back alone… the wind felt colder, and every kilometer felt longer.

But even without a hug or a photo, that day lives forever in my heart — frame by frame, smile by smile.

And next time… we’ll hug a little tighter, smile a little longer, and never forget to click that one perfect picture 📸❤️
